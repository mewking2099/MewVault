# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Product design team — peers, not an external audience. Watching on a large TV in a meeting room, seated 2–4 metres away. Familiar with design process and craft; not familiar with AI tooling or developer workflows.

## Product Purpose

A 30-minute internal talk that shares how design thinking was applied to a personal workflow problem. The goal is not to sell a tool — it is to spark the audience's own thinking about their workflows. Success: someone leaves with one idea they want to try.

## Brand Personality

Warm, honest, personal. A designer sharing real work with peers — not a pitch, not a tutorial. The tone is "let me show you what I built and why" not "here is the future of AI."

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS pitch decks: gradient hero backgrounds, big metric callouts, navy-and-blue corporate palette
- Overly technical slides: code blocks, terminal screenshots, jargon walls
- Conference keynote theatre: dramatic reveals, overly polished corporate feel

## Design Principles

1. **Readable at distance first.** Every size, weight, and contrast decision is made for a TV 3 metres away, not a laptop screen.
2. **Warm over slick.** Amber, not neon. Soft transitions, not sharp reveals. The personality of the content carries — the design stays out of the way.
3. **One job per slide.** Every slide makes one point. Nothing competes for attention.
4. **Human details over system polish.** Real problems, real product names, imperfect phrasing. Credibility comes from specificity, not production value.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- TV display in ambient-light meeting room: minimum effective contrast must account for glare and colour shift
- All body text and bullet text must be clearly legible at 3–4m viewing distance
- No reliance on colour alone to convey meaning (section tags use dot + label, not colour only)
- Reduced motion: slide transitions are opacity-only, safe for motion-sensitive viewers
